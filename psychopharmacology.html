Psychopharmacology & Medication Management

  • Services
  • Psychopharmacology & Medication Management

At Union Square Practice, our psychiatrists and psychiatric nurse practitioners will help diagnose, treat and manage mental, emotional, and behavior disorders. Dr. Michael Dulchin, USP’s co-founder, and Dr. Jessica Stack, child/teen specialist, are board-certified psychiatrists with expertise and experience in medication management and therapy.

Read on or watch our video to learn more about our psychiatrists.
  • Psychiatrist is a medical doctor
  • Completed four years of medical school training 
  • Specialized in the field of psychiatry (post medical school)
  • Can prescribe medication
  • Conduct therapy

Our psychiatry team will work with your current therapist, whether they are an outside provider or a USP clinician.

Some common conditions our psychiatrists treat are:
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Panic disorders
  • Attention Deficit Disorder
  • Personality disorders 

For medication management,we begin with a comprehensive evaluation by either a psychiatrist or psychiatric nurse practitioner. We have expertise and experience in prescribing the full range of psychiatric medications. We know that taking a medication is a very personal choice.  While we offer recommendations, our style is to collaborate with patients to ensure they are well-informed on effects and side-effects.  Whatever you decide, we’re here to work with you through the process.

Medication Management Process:
  • Comprehensive evaluation to understand symptoms
  • Learn if symptom(s) should be treated individually or part of a larger diagnosis
  • Recommend appropriate medications
  • Set follow-up visits to monitor medication
    • In the beginning, follow-ups occur weekly or every other week
    • Later, visits need to occur once every 3 months
  • Recommend therapy
    • Medication with therapy is often more effective than just medication on its own
    • Each case is individual

Our psychiatry team provides both medication management and talk therapy. If you are taking a medication as part of your treatment and also participating in therapy, your therapist may be your prescribing clinican or another therapist at our practice depending on the issue you are working on, specialization and availability. Our Clinical Coordinator will help you choose the appropriate psychiatrist or psychiatric nurse practitioner based on availability, cost and expertise.
